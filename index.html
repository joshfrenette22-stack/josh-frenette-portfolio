The Concept

Most field events compete on the same dimension: venue quality, food, speaker credentials. The result is a market where every invite looks identical and attendance rates reflect it. 02

03
Sales Alignment & Post-Event Pipeline
Every attendee mapped to an open opportunity or target account before the event. Post-event follow-up sequences pre-built and personalized by account tier, launched within 48 hours. AE debrief called within 24 hours to capture conversation intelligence.

Strong pipeline results from Year 1 justified expanding to additional markets in Year 2. Refined the account targeting model based on Year 1 conversion data. Grew total attendance and maintained the format integrity that made it work.

The System

Field marketing programs generate a lot of activity. The Account Exposure & Engagement Matrix is the layer that turns activity into accountability — tracking which accounts were touched, how deeply, and what happened to the pipeline afterward.

Three-Phase Framework
01
Pre-Event Gap Analysis
Before every event, a gap analysis is run against the target account list: which accounts are registered, which are missing, and which require direct outreach escalation. AEs are briefed with account-specific intelligence and talking points pulled from 6sense and Salesforce before they arrive on-site.
02
Live Engagement Tracking
Real-time campaign member tracking during the event against the pre-built target account matrix. Engagement signals (sessions attended, meeting held, booth visit, dinner attendee) are logged and categorized. Each account gets an exposure score based on touchpoint depth.
03
Post-Event Pipeline Readout
A structured pipeline readout is produced within 5 business days: pipeline sourced, opportunities influenced, accounts touched vs. target, and ROI calculation against total program spend. The readout goes to Sales, RevOps, and Marketing leadership with no interpretation — just the numbers.

Forecasting

Bottom-Up GTM Forecast Model

The Problem

Most GTM forecasts are built top-down, finance gives you a number, and marketing reverse-engineers a story. The result is a forecast no one believes and a planning process that wastes months of calendar time.

The Approach

I built a driver-based model anchored to the actual mechanics of the business: how many target accounts exist, what percentage we can reach, how they convert through each stage, and at what ACV. Every assumption is explicit, every number is traceable.

01
Define the addressable universe
Segment TAM by ICP tier, region, and firmographic fit. Build the account list from 6sense and ZoomInfo with coverage scoring applied.
02
Apply conversion rate assumptions
Meeting rate, meeting-to-opp, opp-to-close, and ACV by segment, all benchmarked against trailing four-quarter actuals.
03
Model scenario ranges
Conservative, Base, and Optimistic scenarios let leadership stress-test the plan before committing headcount or spend.
04
Connect to the operating cadence
Model feeds the weekly pipeline council review. Actuals vs. model tracked in real time; assumptions updated as data accumulates through the quarter.
Outcomes
Forecast accuracy improved to 94% Board reporting simplified Planning cycle reduced 6 weeks Headcount decisions grounded in model

Bowtie Funnel Intelligence

The Problem

Most funnel analysis stops at closed-won. That misses half the revenue picture. Expansion, renewal, and churn are driven by the same buyer signals that show up in the pre-sales funnel, if you know how to read them.

The Framework

The bowtie model maps the full customer journey: anonymous awareness through post-sale expansion. Each stage has defined entry and exit criteria, a conversion rate, and an owner. Marketing owns the left side; CS owns the right; RevOps owns the data layer.

What It Surfaces
Top-of-Funnel Efficiency
Which channels produce accounts that actually close, not just leads that generate MQLs.
Stage Conversion Leaks
Where qualified pipeline stalls, and whether the root cause is timing, fit, or competitive displacement.
Expansion Signals
Engagement patterns that predict upsell readiness before the CSM conversation happens.
Churn Early Warning
Drops in product engagement, support ticket patterns, and contact map changes that precede a cancellation.
Outcomes
Full-funnel visibility established Attribution confidence improved Marketing contribution to expansion tracked

AI / GTM Intelligence

Custom LLM Pipeline Intelligence Engine

The Problem

AEs were spending six or more hours per week on manual account research: pulling intent data, reading news alerts, reviewing CRM history, and writing outreach. The information existed. It just lived in six different places with no synthesis layer.

The Build

I built a custom intelligence engine that connects our CRM, intent platform, and news feeds into a unified prompt architecture. The LLM synthesizes account signals into a concise brief, including pipeline risk score, recent news, key contacts, competitive flags, and a suggested opening angle, delivered to the AE in Slack before each call.

01
Data ingestion layer
Pulls from Salesforce opportunity data, 6sense intent signals, news API, and LinkedIn Sales Navigator activity alerts.
02
LLM synthesis
Custom prompt chain synthesizes signals into a structured account brief. Risk flags, momentum indicators, and recommended next actions included.
03
Delivery via Slack
Briefs delivered automatically before each scheduled discovery call. AE feedback loop improves prompt accuracy over time.
Results
6 hrs/week saved per AE Discovery call quality improved Pipeline risk surfaced earlier 82% AE adoption in 30 days
